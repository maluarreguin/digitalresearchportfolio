Digital Storytelling

Final Project
Created for Digital Humanities 250: Digital Storytelling
Spring 2022

Link to Project

For my Final Project in my Digital Storytelling course, I was tasked with creating a final project that encompassed some form of a digital narrative, while also utilizing the skillsets we had learned and implemented through class. For this, I decided to merge multiple concepts I had been stewing with over the Fall Quarter of 2022 and merge them into one compounding piece, thus, leading us to my project, Again and Again and Again. My end goal for this project was to incorporate the idea of storytelling and integrate that into a digital medium that could be both versatile and adaptable with time. I also chose to write on a subject matter that, at its core, is a bit more personal as a healthy mechanism for me to work through some thoughts and feelings that I’ve been holding onto. Ideally, I wanted to turn these concepts on their head and create something that was distinct, imputing a new perspective on my private endeavors. Transforming my old relationship into a meta-narrative about the gamification of modern dating was a fun and exciting project to delve into, and remains a topic I’m constantly thinking about and engrossed by. I feel as though because I’m discussing digital games and the online medium, it makes sense that my piece is on a website, as through this form I was able to incorporate images and small thematic features that highlight the narrative of the essay at large. I hope one day that I can revisit this piece and make it more interactive and creative to further exemplify the story at large.
